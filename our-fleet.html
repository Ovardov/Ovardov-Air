A380

The double-deck A380 makes excellent use of its space to offer passengers attainable luxury. With enough room to install stylish first-class suites.

A340

The A340 provides some of the most comfortable environments aloft, well suited for long- and ultra-long haul flights of up to 18 hours.

A330

The A330 is a more efficient aircraft that will generate savings through its reduced fuel burn. It includes latest-generation Rolls-Royce Trent 7000 engines.

A321

The Airbus 321 engine provides a 20% reduction in fuel consumption per seat, 50% noise and pollution reduction compared to previous generation aircraft.

A220

The A220 Family is ideal for hot-and-high and city-centre airport operations. Wide seats combined with ample overhead storage.

Boeing 787

The Boeing 787 can fly up to 7,635 nautical miles (14,140 km), while reducing fuel use and emissions by 20 to 25 percent compared to older airplanes.

Boeing 767

We love the Boeing 767 because of its usefulness on short haul, high-density routes. Routes such as Chicago to New York feature the aircraft heavily.
